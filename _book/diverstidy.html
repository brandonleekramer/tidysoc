4.2 Geographic Analysis

4.2.1 detect_countries()

Sources for output parameters:

  1. country: Country names based on International Organization for Standardization with all punctuation removed to allow names to be more readable (ISO).
  2. iso_2: International Organization for Standardization’s two-digit country codes (ISO). NOTE: The CIA World Factbook uses a Geopolitical Entities and Codes (GEC) (Formerly FIPS 1PUB 10-4) schema that is now based on the ISO-2.
  3. iso_3: International Organization for Standardization’s three-digit country codes (ISO). NOTE: The CIA World Factbook uses a Letter Codes for Geographical Entities Standardization Agreement (STANAG) that is based on the ISO-3.
  4. iso_numeric: International Organization for Standardization’s three-digit numeric codes (ISO) NOTE: The United Nations also uses the ISO numeric code as their M49 area codes.
  5. iso_domain: International Organization for Standardization’s three-digit numeric codes (ISO)
  6. continent: Continents that each country is located based onon International Organization for Standardization (ISO).
  7. region: Regions each country are in based on the United Nations’ M49 classification.
  8. sub_region: Subregions each country are in based on the United Nations’ M49 classification.
  9. int_region: Intermediary regions each country are in based on the United Nations’ M49 classification.
  10. country_english: Country names based verbatim on International Organization for Standardization (ISO).
  11. country_chinese: Country names in Chinese as translated by the United Nations.
  12. country_russian: Country names in Russian as translated by the United Nations.
  13. country_french: Country names in French as translated by the United Nations.
  14. country_spanish: Country names in Spanish as translated by the United Nations.
  15. country_arabic: Country names in Arabic as translated by the United Nations.
  16. latitude and longitude: Averaged latitude and longitude taken from Gapminder
